1.Banana Walnut Brownie

In a large glass bowl, take 2 medium-sized bananas and mash them using a fork.When it is properly mashed, add 2 raw eggs to it.
Whisk the mixture properly till a smooth lump-free consistency is achieved.
Now add rolled oats to the mixture. Mix again.
Add molten unsalted white butter to the mixture.
Further, put some baking soda to it, to help the brownie rise. Also, add some vanilla essence.
Lastly, add cocoa powder to the mixture. Mix everything properly. If you want your brownie to be more chocolaty, you can increase the amount of cocoa powder in it. Let's start with the baking process.
Take a baking tray/dish of the desired shape. Grease its bottom with oil or line it with butter paper.
Now add the brownie mixture to the baking tray and make it uniform using a spatula.
Sprinkle some finely chopped walnuts on top.
Now preheat the oven to 180° celsius for 10 minutes.
Place the tray in the oven for baking for 30 minutes at the same temperature i.e., 180° celsius.
After 30 minutes, take the tray out of the oven, let it cool down and carefully remove the brownie from the tray.
Cut it into small pieces and serve.

2.Egg Tikka

Boil 5 eggs in a pan for 10-15 minutes.
Mix all the masalas and add some water to them. Make a thick consistency.
Now heat the pan and add 1/2 tsp olive oil.
Then dip the boiled eggs one by one into the batter and put it on the pan.
Let it cook from all sides and serve hot you can also sprinkle some chaat masala and pickled onion.

3.High Protein Moonglet

Soak moong dal overnight. The next day, grind to a coarse paste without adding water.
Mix the batter well with salt and chopped veggies. Also add heeng, black pepper and some ground flaxseeds. Mix everything to make a fluffy batter.
Heat a heavy-bottomed pan with a generous amount of ghee.
Pour 2 ladles full of batter to make a thick (pancake/ chilla).
Cook covered patiently to give a golden crust.
Flip carefully and continue to cook on the other side. Slit open with a spatula the chilla into 4 parts and smear ghee as needed.
When both sides are golden and crispy, take out and serve immediately.

4.Palak Patta Chat

Take a cup of besan in a bowl and add salt, chilli powder, ajwain, and water.
Whisk it to make a smooth batter. Add more water if needed. Put 1/4 of turmeric in it and mix again.
Now take fresh and cleaned palak leaves, coat them entirely with the besan mix, and put them in an air fryer at 200 degrees for 10 minutes
Put the crispy palak leaves on a plate and pour some curd all over them.
Take green chutney and start garnishing the dish with it.
Now sprinkle some chaat masala according to your taste.
Lastly, garnish with coriander leaves, garlic, beetroot, seed mix, and pomegranate seeds for a blend of sweet and spicy taste.

5.Rotti Pizza

To make the pizza, take a large semi-cooked roti as a pizza base and make pizza sauce by mixing red chilli powder, chaat masala, salt and tandoori masala with greek yoghurt.
Now add toppings like chopped red and yellow bell peppers, sliced baby corn, finely sliced onions and half-cut cherry tomatoes. Also, add a few basil leaves on top.
Now take grated paneer and malai and use them as cheese by pouring it all over the pizza.
Preheat the oven to 150-degree celsius and bake the roti pizza for 10 minutes.
Your pizza is ready to eat hot! Sprinkle some oregano and chilli flakes on top if you want.
